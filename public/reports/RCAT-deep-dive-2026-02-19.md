# Red Cat Holdings (RCAT) - Deep Dive Research Report
*Research Date: February 19, 2026*

## Executive Summary

Red Cat Holdings (NASDAQ: RCAT) is a U.S.-based drone technology company that has emerged as a key player in military/defense UAV systems. The company has experienced explosive revenue growth driven by major defense contracts, particularly winning the U.S. Army's Short Range Reconnaissance (SRR) program over competitor Skydio. Despite strong revenue momentum, RCAT remains deeply unprofitable with significant cash burn, making it a high-risk, high-reward investment thesis centered on defense spending and American-made drone adoption.

---

## 1. Business Overview

### Core Business
- **Primary Focus**: Military, government, and public safety drone systems
- **Key Subsidiaries**: Teal Drones and FlightWave Aerospace  
- **Geographic Base**: Puerto Rico (manufacturing), targeting U.S. government clients
- **Product Portfolio**: "Family of Systems" led by Black Widow™ drone platform

### Market Position
- **Differentiation**: American-made hardware addressing "Blue UAS List" requirements
- **Strategic Advantage**: Domestic manufacturing amid geopolitical tensions with China
- **Target Markets**: U.S. Army, Department of Defense, allied nations
- **Key Programs**: Short Range Reconnaissance (SRR) program winner

### Competitive Landscape
**Primary Competitors:**
- **AeroVironment (AVAV)**: Market leader, larger scale, established defense relationships
- **Skydio**: Private company, $2.2B valuation, lost SRR contract to RCAT
- **Anduril Industries**: Private, $14B valuation, broader defense technology focus
- **Teledyne FLIR**: Larger conglomerate with drone capabilities
- **Shield AI**: Private, autonomous drone systems

**Competitive Positioning:**
- RCAT positioned as scrappy challenger with government contract wins
- Smaller scale but agile, focusing specifically on military UAV segment
- Benefits from "Buy American" defense procurement trends
