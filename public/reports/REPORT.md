## The Business

Unusual Machines designs, manufactures, and sells drone components and complete drone systems, with a focus on **NDAA-compliant** (National Defense Authorization Act) products for U.S. defense and government applications.

**Key assets:**
- **Fat Shark** — Ultra-low latency FPV video goggles for drone pilots (leading brand in the space)
- **Rotor Riot** — NDAA-compliant drone components and systems
- Orlando, FL headquarters

**How they make money:**
1. **Consumer/Prosumer** — FPV goggles and components for drone enthusiasts
2. **B2B/Defense** — NDAA-compliant components for government contractors and defense applications
3. **Strategic investments** — Stakes in XTEND ($1.5B merger) and Safe Pro Group

The company is pivoting from primarily consumer FPV products toward **defense/government contracts** — a much larger TAM with better margins.

## Competitive Position

**Moat assessment: MODERATE**

**Strengths:**
- **NDAA compliance** — Critical for U.S. government contracts; Chinese competitors (DJI) are effectively banned
- **Fat Shark brand** — Dominant in FPV goggles, loyal enthusiast base
- **Trump connection** — Donald Trump Jr. on advisory board; strategic investment alongside Eric Trump in XTEND merger
- **First-mover in domestic drone supply chain** — Positioned as key supplier as U.S. "reshores" drone manufacturing

**Weaknesses:**
- Still small revenue base ($7.7M TTM)
- Consumer FPV market is niche
- Execution risk on defense pivot
- Competition from other NDAA-compliant players emerging

**Competitors:**
- AgEagle (drone systems)
- Red Cat Holdings (Teal drones)
- Skydio (stealth, VC-backed)
- Emerging domestic players
