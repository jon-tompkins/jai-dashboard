# Wyckoff Frameworks — Smart Money Analysis

## Core Principles

### The Three Laws
1. **Supply and Demand** — Price moves when supply/demand is unbalanced
2. **Cause and Effect** — Accumulation/distribution (cause) creates trends (effect)  
3. **Effort vs Result** — Volume should confirm price moves

### The Composite Operator Concept
Think like institutional players who:
- Accumulate quietly during weakness
- Distribute during strength and public euphoria
- Create false moves to trap retail traders
- Leave footprints in price and volume patterns

## Specific Setups/Patterns

### Accumulation Phases (Smart Money Buying)

**Phase A - Stopping Action**
- Preliminary Support (PS): First significant low with volume
- Selling Climax (SC): Panic selling, highest volume, wide spread
- Automatic Reaction (AR): Natural bounce after climax

**Phase B - Building Cause** 
- Multiple Secondary Tests (ST): Retesting lows on lower volume
- Trading Range formation: Smart money quietly accumulating
- Look for: Lower volume on down moves, higher volume on up moves

**Phase C - Testing**
- Spring: False breakdown below support, quickly reverses (trap retail shorts)
- Test: Low volume retest of spring low confirms strength
- Last Point of Support (LPS): Final low before markup

**Phase D - Evidence of Demand**
- Sign of Strength (SOS): Break above range on high volume
- Backup: Pullback to range resistance (now support)
- Higher highs and higher lows begin

**Phase E - Markup**
- Sustained uptrend as institutions complete their positions
- Public awareness increases

### Distribution Phases (Smart Money Selling)

**Phase A - Initial Supply**
- Preliminary Supply (PSY): First sign of selling pressure
- Buying Climax (BC): Emotional buying peak, wide spread, high volume
- Automatic Reaction (AR): Initial pullback

**Phase B - Building Effect**
- Secondary Tests: Retesting highs on lower volume
- Upthrust After Distribution (UTAD): False breakout above range
- Smart money distributing to eager retail buyers

**Phase C - Final Testing**
- Upthrust: False break above resistance, quickly reverses
- Test of UTAD: Confirms supply, sets up distribution

**Phase D - Evidence of Supply**
- Sign of Weakness (SOW): Break below range support
- Last Point of Supply (LPSY): Final rally before decline

**Phase E - Markdown**
- Sustained downtrend as supply overwhelms demand

### Key Patterns

**Springs (Accumulation)**
- Price dips below support level
- Reverses quickly back into range
- Shows supply is exhausted
- Often triggers stop losses, creating liquidity for smart money

**Upthrusts (Distribution)**
- Price breaks above resistance
- Fails to hold, reverses back into range  
- Shows demand is weak
- Traps breakout buyers

## How to Apply to Chart Reading

### Volume Analysis Priority
1. **Climactic Volume** = Potential turning point
2. **Diminishing Volume** on tests = Strength building
3. **Volume Confirmation** = Real breakouts have volume
4. **Volume Divergence** = Question the move

### Structure Recognition
1. **Identify the Phase** — Where in accumulation/distribution cycle?
2. **Count the Cause** — Wider/longer range = bigger effect
3. **Measure the Effect** — Range width projects move size
4. **Watch for Tests** — Do retests hold with less volume?

### Entry Timing
- **Late Phase C**: After successful spring/test
- **Early Phase D**: On first SOS with volume
- **Phase E**: Trend continuation with pullbacks to structure

### Exit Signals
- **Climactic Action**: Buying/selling climax suggests end
- **No Follow-Through**: Breakout without volume
- **Distribution Signs**: Upthrusts and volume divergence

## Example Scenarios

### Accumulation Example
```
1. Stock drops from $50 to $30 on high volume (SC)
2. Bounces to $35, then tests $30 again on lower volume (ST)
3. Range forms between $30-35 for several months
4. False break to $28, immediately recovers (Spring)
5. Breaks $35 on high volume (SOS) 
6. Pullback to $35 holds (LPS)
7. Trend begins toward $50+ target (cause = $5 range, effect = $20 move)
```

### Distribution Example
```
1. Stock rallies from $30 to $50 on emotional buying (BC)
2. Pulls back to $45, then retests $50 on lower volume
3. Range forms between $45-50 for months
4. False break to $52, immediately fails (Upthrust)
5. Breaks $45 on volume (SOW)
6. Rally back to $47 fails (LPSY)
7. Trend begins toward $25 target
```

### Spring Identification
```
Chart shows:
- Clear support at $100
- Break to $98 on moderate volume
- Immediate recovery back above $100 same day/week
- Next test of $100 holds with less volume
- Breakout above range resistance

Action: Spring confirmed, accumulation complete, go long on SOS
```

### Volume Divergence Warning
```
Price making new highs at $75, $77, $80
Volume decreasing: 1M, 800K, 500K shares
No follow-through after $80 high
Wide spread reversal bar appears

Interpretation: Distribution likely, prepare for reversal
```

## Key Questions for Every Chart

1. **What phase are we in?** Accumulation, distribution, trend?
2. **Where's the composite operator?** Buying weakness or selling strength?
3. **Does volume confirm price?** Real move or manipulation?
4. **Is this a test?** Spring, upthrust, or genuine breakout?
5. **What's the cause?** How much range justifies how much move?
