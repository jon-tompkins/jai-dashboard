# Linda Raschke Momentum — Precision Timing Systems

## Core Principles

### Momentum Precedes Price
- Momentum indicators often diverge before price reverses
- Look for momentum making new highs/lows when price doesn't
- Early warning system for trend changes
- Use oscillators to time entries in established trends

### ADX for Trend Strength
**ADX above 30**: Strong trend in place
- Trade with the trend, buy pullbacks in uptrends
- Avoid counter-trend trades
- Look for first pullback to moving average

**ADX below 20**: Choppy, range-bound market  
- Fade breakouts, they'll likely fail
- Trade range-bound strategies
- Wait for ADX to rise before trending trades

**ADX rising**: Trend is strengthening
**ADX falling**: Trend is weakening, prepare for range

### First Thrust Principle
- First breakout of a range often fails
- Market needs to "test" the breakout
- Better trades come on second attempt or pullback
- Patience pays off more than chasing breakouts

## Specific Setups/Patterns

### Holy Grail Setup (HG)
**Prerequisites:**
- 14-period ADX > 30 and rising (strong trend confirmed)
- Clear trend direction established
- Price pulls back to 20 EMA for first time

**Entry Rules:**
- Must be FIRST pullback to 20 EMA after trend establishment
- Enter when price bounces off 20 EMA with momentum
- No second or third touches (reduces probability)
- Strong trend must remain intact (ADX still > 30)

**Management:**
- Stop loss: Below recent swing low (uptrend) or above swing high (downtrend)
- Target: Next resistance/support or measured move
- Trail stops as trend continues

### Turtle Soup (Failed Breakout Reversal)
**Concept:** Fade quick failures of significant breakouts
Named after "Turtle" trend followers who get trapped

**Setup Requirements:**
- Significant support/resistance level (20-day high/low minimum)
- Clean breakout above/below this level
- Immediate failure back into range (same day or next)
- Volume confirmation on reversal

**Entry Method:**
- Short failed upside breakouts below the breakout bar low
- Buy failed downside breakouts above the breakout bar high
- Enter close to the failure, not after big reversal move

**Risk Management:**
- Tight stops just beyond the failed breakout point
- Quick profits - don't overstay, ranges don't last forever

### Anti Pattern (Turtle Soup Plus One)
- Failed breakout that fails again the next day
- Even higher probability setup
- Enter on second failure for maximum confidence

### First Thrust Pattern
**Observation:** Markets often fail on first attempt to break range

**Application:**
- When price breaks range for first time, be skeptical
- Wait for pullback and second attempt
- Or fade the first breakout entirely
- Second breakout attempts have higher success rate

### 3/10 Oscillator Trades
**Setup:** 3-period MA of close minus 10-period MA of close
**Signals:**
- **Above Zero Line + Rising**: Momentum bullish
- **Below Zero Line + Falling**: Momentum bearish
- **Divergence**: Price new high/low, oscillator doesn't confirm
- **Extreme Readings**: Potential reversal zones

### NR7 (Narrow Range 7)
**Definition:** Today's range is smaller than previous 6 days
**Interpretation:** Volatility compression, explosion coming
**Trading:** Enter on break of NR7 bar in either direction
**Stop:** Other side of NR7 bar
**Target:** Measured move based on recent average ranges

## How to Apply to Chart Reading

### Trend Analysis Sequence
1. **Check ADX Level**: Above 30 = trend, below 20 = range
2. **Identify Trend Direction**: Price vs moving averages
3. **Count Pullbacks**: First pullback = opportunity, multiple = weakening
4. **Check for Divergences**: Price vs momentum oscillators

### Entry Timing Process
**For Trend Trades:**
1. Confirm ADX > 30 and trend direction
2. Wait for pullback to 20 EMA (first one preferred)
3. Look for momentum bounce signal (3/10 oscillator, RSI, etc.)
4. Enter with tight stop below pullback low

**For Range Trades:**
1. Confirm ADX < 20 (choppy market)
2. Identify range boundaries
3. Look for failed breakouts (Turtle Soup setups)
4. Fade the failures with tight stops

**For Breakout Trades:**
1. Identify narrow range compression (NR7)
2. Wait for breakout in either direction
3. Enter with stop on opposite side
4. Trail stops as momentum continues

### Momentum Divergence Analysis
**Bullish Divergence:**
- Price makes lower low
- Momentum oscillator makes higher low
- Suggests selling pressure exhausted

**Bearish Divergence:**
- Price makes higher high  
- Momentum oscillator makes lower high
- Suggests buying pressure exhausted

**Hidden Divergences:**
- Trend continuation signals
- Price pullback while momentum stays strong
- Enter in direction of main trend

## Example Scenarios

### Holy Grail Long Setup
```
Stock in strong uptrend, ADX = 35 and rising
Price has moved from $50 to $60 without major pullback
First pullback to 20 EMA occurs at $57
3/10 oscillator bounces from oversold
Signal bar closes strong above 20 EMA

Entry: Buy above signal bar high ($57.50)
Stop: Below pullback low ($56.50)  
Target: Prior high ($60) or measured move ($64)
```

### Turtle Soup Short Setup
```
Stock breaks above 20-day high at $75
Same day, reverses and closes back below $75
Next day, gaps down and continues lower
Signal: Failed breakout confirmed

Entry: Short below failure day low ($74.50)
Stop: Above breakout high ($75.25)
Target: Range support around $70
```

### NR7 Breakout
```
Stock trading $40-45 range for weeks
Yesterday's range: $42.10-$42.90 (smallest in 7 days)
Today breaks above $42.90 on increased volume
Signal: Volatility expansion beginning

Entry: Long above $42.90
Stop: Below $42.10
Target: $45 (range top) or $47 (measured move)
```

### ADX Trend Change
```
ADX declining from 40 to 25 over past week
Previously strong uptrend now making sideways moves
Price testing moving averages repeatedly
Signal: Trend weakening, enter range mode

Action: Stop looking for trend continuation
Start looking for range-bound fades
Wait for ADX to bottom and rise again for next trend
```

### Momentum Divergence
```
Price rallies from $30 to $35 to $38 (new highs)
RSI readings: 75, 65, 55 (declining)
Volume also declining on each rally
Signal: Bearish momentum divergence

Setup: Look for reversal patterns near $38
Enter short on break of recent support
Target return to $32-35 area
```

## Advanced Applications

### Multiple Timeframe Analysis
- **Long-term trend** (daily/weekly): Sets overall direction
- **Intermediate pullback** (hourly): Provides entry timing  
- **Short-term momentum** (15-min): Confirms entry signal

### Opening Range Strategy
- Mark first 30-60 minutes range
- Trade breakouts from opening range
- First thrust often fails (fade it)
- Second thrust more reliable (trade with it)

### Gap Analysis with Momentum
- **Gap up with strong momentum**: Likely to hold and extend
- **Gap up with weak momentum**: Likely to fill
- **Gap down in strong uptrend**: Buy the dip opportunity
- Use momentum indicators to gauge gap quality

### Market Structure Integration
**Strong Trends (ADX > 30):**
- Buy first pullback in uptrend
- Sell first rally in downtrend
- Don't fight the momentum

**Weak Trends (ADX < 20):**
- Fade all breakouts initially
- Look for Turtle Soup setups
- Trade ranges until momentum returns

## Key Decision Points

### Before Every Trade Ask:
1. **What's the ADX reading?** Trend or range environment?
2. **Is this the first thrust or second attempt?**
3. **Are momentum and price confirming each other?**
4. **Where's the logical stop loss?**
5. **Is risk/reward at least 1:2?**

### Exit Strategy Rules:
- **In trends**: Trail stops, let winners run
- **In ranges**: Take quick profits, don't get greedy
- **On divergences**: Exit when momentum confirms reversal
- **On failed setups**: Exit immediately, don't hope
