# Al Brooks Price Action — Reading Bars Like Language

## Core Principles

### Every Bar Tells a Story
- **Bull Bar**: Close above open, bulls won that time period
- **Bear Bar**: Close below open, bears won that time period  
- **Doji/Inside Bar**: Uncertainty, balance between bulls and bears
- **Outside Bar**: Breakout attempt, expansion of range

### Always-In Concept
Market is ALWAYS in one of these states:
- **Always-In Long**: Trend up, buy pullbacks, avoid shorts
- **Always-In Short**: Trend down, sell rallies, avoid longs  
- **Trading Range**: Fade extremes, buy low sell high

The market transitions between these states. Your job: identify which state you're in.

### The 80% Rule
- 80% of bars are in trading ranges
- 80% of breakout attempts fail
- 80% of trend attempts become trading ranges
- Only 20% of moves become strong trends

This means: **Fade first breakouts, wait for second signal**

### Measured Moves
Trends tend to create equal legs:
- First leg up from A to B
- Pullback from B to C  
- Second leg targets B-A distance from C
- Third leg often equals first two combined

## Specific Setups/Patterns

### Signal Bars and Entry Bars

**Signal Bar**: The setup bar that creates the trade opportunity
**Entry Bar**: The next bar where you actually enter

**Perfect Signal Bar Criteria:**
- Strong close (near high for bulls, near low for bears)
- Good size body (not a doji)
- Closes beyond prior swing point
- Preferably breaks trend line or moving average

**Entry Methods:**
- **Aggressive**: Enter during signal bar
- **Conservative**: Enter above/below signal bar high/low
- **Limit Order**: Enter at moving average or support/resistance

### High Probability Setups

**High 1 (H1)**
- Signal bar: Bull bar breaking above prior bar
- Entry: Buy above signal bar high
- Stop: Below signal bar low

**High 2 (H2)** 
- Two consecutive bull bars
- Entry: Buy above second bar
- Higher probability than H1

**Low 1 (L1) / Low 2 (L2)**
- Mirror image for bear setups
- Sell below bear signal bars

**Wedge Patterns**
- Three pushes in same direction with diverging momentum
- Third push often fails
- Trade reversal after third push

**Failed Breakout**
- Strong move in one direction
- Immediate reversal back into range
- High probability counter-trend trade

### Trading Range Strategies

**Buy Setup in Range:**
- Near bottom of range
- After 2-3 bear bars (selling climax)
- Signal bar that closes above its open
- Target: Top of range

**Sell Setup in Range:**
- Near top of range  
- After 2-3 bull bars (buying climax)
- Signal bar that closes below its open
- Target: Bottom of range

**Breakout Trades:**
- Wait for second entry (first often fails)
- Strong close beyond range
- Follow-through bar confirms
- Target: Measured move

## How to Apply to Chart Reading

### Reading Context First
1. **What's the overall trend?** (Higher timeframe)
2. **Are we in trend or range?** (Current timeframe)
3. **Where in the range are we?** (Top, middle, bottom)
4. **What's the recent momentum?** (Consecutive bars same color)

### Bar-by-Bar Analysis Process

**For Each Bar Ask:**
1. **Strong or weak bar?** (Size of body, where it closed)
2. **Does it confirm or contradict prior bars?**
3. **Is this a potential signal bar?**
4. **What would bulls/bears do here?**

**Reading Combinations:**
- **Two strong bull bars**: Trend likely continues
- **Bull bar, doji, bull bar**: Pause in uptrend, probably continues
- **Three consecutive bear bars**: Selling pressure, trend change possible
- **Outside bar after trend**: Potential reversal

### Entry Timing Rules

**With the Trend:**
- Buy pullbacks to moving average in uptrend
- Sell rallies to moving average in downtrend
- Enter on first pullback after strong breakout

**Counter-Trend (Range Trading):**
- Need strong reversal setup (wedge, climax, failed breakout)
- Enter quickly when setup triggers
- Take profits quickly (ranges don't last forever)

**Breakout Trading:**
- Avoid first breakout attempts (usually fail)
- Wait for pullback and second attempt
- Or wait for strong follow-through after breakout

## Example Scenarios

### Always-In Long Example
```
Daily Chart: Series of higher highs, higher lows
5-min Chart: Looking for pullbacks to 20 EMA
Signal: Bull bar bouncing off 20 EMA
Entry: Buy above signal bar high
Stop: Below recent swing low
Target: Prior high or measured move
```

### Trading Range Fade
```
Stock in $100-$110 range for weeks
Price hits $109.50 after three consecutive bull bars
Signal: Bear reversal bar with tail at top
Entry: Sell below signal bar low  
Stop: Above signal bar high
Target: $105 (middle of range) or $100 (bottom)
```

### Failed Breakout
```
Strong rally breaks above $50 resistance
Next bar: Large bear bar that closes back below $50
Signal: Failed breakout confirmed
Entry: Short below bear bar low
Stop: Above breakout high ($50.50)
Target: Recent swing low or measured move down
```

### Measured Move Target
```
Rally from $40 to $50 (first leg = $10)
Pullback to $47 (correction)
Second leg up should target $47 + $10 = $57
Enter long on pullback to $47 area
```

### Wedge Reversal
```
Three pushes up: $45→$48, $47→$49, $48.50→$49.25
Each push weaker (smaller, less follow-through)
Third push makes marginal new high
Signal: Strong bear reversal bar after third push
Entry: Short below reversal bar
Target: Back to wedge bottom ($45-46)
```

## Advanced Concepts

### Bar Counting
- Count consecutive bars in same direction
- After 3+ bars, expect pause or reversal
- Climax patterns: 5+ bars often lead to reversal

### Gap Analysis
- **Measuring Gap**: Middle of trend, resume trend direction
- **Breakaway Gap**: Start of trend, fade back to gap is buying/selling opportunity  
- **Exhaustion Gap**: End of trend, often gets filled quickly

### Moving Average Interaction
- **Above MA in uptrend**: Support, buy pullbacks
- **Below MA in downtrend**: Resistance, sell rallies
- **Crossing MA**: Trend change signal, but wait for confirmation

### Momentum vs. Trend
- **Strong momentum bars**: Trend likely continues
- **Weak momentum** (dojis, overlapping bars): Trend losing steam
- **Divergence**: Price makes new high/low, but bars getting weaker

## Decision Tree for Every Bar

1. **Trend or Range?**
   - If trend: Trade with pullbacks
   - If range: Fade extremes

2. **Strong or Weak Setup?**
   - Strong: Enter on signal
   - Weak: Wait for better setup

3. **With or Against Always-In?**
   - With: Higher probability
   - Against: Need very strong reversal signal

4. **First or Second Entry?**
   - First: Often fails
   - Second: Higher probability
