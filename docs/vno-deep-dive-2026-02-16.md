## The Business

Vornado is Manhattan's office landlord with a massive concentration risk disguised as a competitive advantage. They own approximately 24-26 million square feet of prime NYC commercial real estate, including office buildings and street-level retail. The company generates revenue through rental income from office leases (primary driver), retail space, and residential units.

The business model is straightforward: own premier real estate in the most expensive markets, collect rent, and pray that NYC office demand never structurally declines. Their crown jewel is the Penn District development, where they're betting big on transforming the area around Penn Station. They also own trophy assets like 1 Beacon Court and 220 Central Park South.

What's concerning? This is a single-market concentration play masquerading as diversification. When NYC office goes down, VNO has nowhere to hide.
